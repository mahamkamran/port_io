Integrated Vortex-Odor Dynamics

Examining the influence of fish schooling on the suppression or dispersion of odor in the vortical wakes.

M. Kamran*, C. Li, and M.S.U Khalid

MSc. Thesis Research @ NIERL, Lakehead University, Canada

Supervisor: Dr. Muhammad Saif Ullah Khalid, P.Eng

Research Collaboration: Dr. Chengyu Li, CWRU, USA

This research investigates the collective effect of fish schooling on odor dispersion using 3D CFD simulations. Results show that more swimmers increase lateral odor dispersion while downstream propagation plateaus beyond a spacing threshold. Odor effectiveness increases linearly with swimmer count in the wake.

3D Coupled Vortex-Odor Dynamics

On the role of morphology and kinematics of biological swimmers to spread and suppress their odors in the wake

Maham Kamran*, A. Fardi, C. Li, and M.S.U Khalid

MSc. Thesis Research @ NIERL, Lakehead University, Canada

Supervisor: Dr. Muhammad Saif Ullah Khalid, P.Eng

Research Collaboration: Dr. Chengyu Li, CWRU, USA

This study explores odor dispersion in fish wakes using high-fidelity 3D CFD with an immersed-boundary method. Results show kinematics dominate odor transport over morphology. Anguilliform swimmers generate broader, more persistent odor trails. Increased undulation amplitude enhances convection-driven odor strength.

2D Coupled Vortex-Odor Dynamics

How does vortex dynamics help undulating bodies spread odor? [DOI: 10.1063/5.0235768]

Maham Kamran*, A. Fardi, C. Li, and M.S.U Khalid

MSc. Thesis Research @ NIERL, Lakehead University, Canada

Supervisor: Dr. Muhammad Saif Ullah Khalid, P.Eng

Research Collaboration: Dr. Chengyu Li, CWRU, USA

This paper uses CFD to study odor and vortex coupling in undulating swimmers. Carangiform and anguilliform motions are compared in air and water. Odor spots align partially with vortices. Convection dominates odor transport in water, while both convection and diffusion matter in air. The study informs AUV design using bio-inspired principles.

Aerothermal Design Analysis of Aircraft

Maham Kamran*, Dr. Ali Sarosh

BASc. Thesis Research @ Shock and Stars Pvt Ltd, Air University, Pakistan

Supervisor: Dr. Ali Sarosh

This project analyzes avionics in unconditioned bays under military standards. Thermal performance of SDR was evaluated using lumped mass models and CFD. Findings show cooling depends on altitude; hot spots are identified. Results support better thermal designs for defense avionics.
